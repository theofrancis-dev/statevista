General Information

The smallest state in the United States, is a land of captivating contrasts, blending rich history with a vibrant contemporary spirit. Nicknamed "The Ocean State," its coastline is dotted with charming towns, picturesque harbors, and stunning beaches, offering a wealth of recreational opportunities. Beyond its shores, Rhode Island boasts a legacy steeped in the American Revolution, with historic sites and landmarks that whisper tales of independence and innovation. From the bustling capital city of Providence, a hub of arts and culture, to the opulent mansions of Newport, reminders of the Gilded Age, Rhode Island offers a diverse tapestry of experiences within its compact borders, making it a compelling destination for those seeking both relaxation and discovery.

Geography

  • R.I. is 48 miles long and 37 miles wide
  • It's bordered by Massachusetts to the north and east, Connecticut to the west, and the Atlantic Ocean to the south
  • has a 400-mile shoreline on Narragansett Bay
  • It includes the islands of Block Island and Narragansett Bay

Economy

History

  • was the first colony to declare independence
  • was the last original colony to join the Union
  • In 1636, Roger Williams founded the colony
  • In 1764, Brown University became the first American college to accept students of all religions.
